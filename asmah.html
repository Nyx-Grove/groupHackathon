GREEN RIBBON GUIDE

If someone has a cognitive impairment it means that they have an intellectual disability. This means that this person would develop certain skills at a slower rate when compared to a neurotypical. Examples of this are autism, adhd, and Alzheimer’s.

Living with a cognitive disability can be very difficult. According to webaim.org, people with Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder, also known as ADHD, have trouble learning because they are easily distracted. What if there was a way to help people with these disabilities so it doesn’t become a burden to them? In Green Ribbon Guide (GRG), this is our goal. To help people learn how to work with their disabilities. Having an intellectual disability is not something one should be ashamed of at all, we just want to ensure that there are resources available, so that these people can feel more comfortable with their disability.
